98. Study of different possible side chain conformations according to the backbone using a structural alphabet


Romain Gautier, 1 Anne-Claude Camproux, 2 Pierre Tuffery 3



Keywords: Side chain conformation, rotamers librairies, entropy

Abstract

The dependence between the backbone conformation and the side chains conformations has been noticed long ago.[1] Dunbrack and Karplus [2] have proposed a rotamer library that takes into account the dependence between the / values of the amino acid and side chain conformations. The generalization of such analysis to larger peptidic fragments is difficult because of the large number of possible conformations to describe the backbone conformation.[3]

Recent development of the concept of structural alphabet [4] allowed to obtain a set of the peptide conformations (Short Structural Building Block, SBBs) that optimally describes proteins backbone conformations.
We have built a new rotamers libraries SBBs-dependent: for each SBBs and for the succession of two SBBs (lib2L). Preferential conformations of the side chains were determined by the research of the modes of the smoothed distribution in space.
Our results show the existence of a significant dependence between the letters of the structural alphabet and the side chains conformations, the average number of rotamers decreases by seven between the backbone independent rotamer library and lib2L.

The analysis of side chain conformations and their frequencies, using both new rotamers libraries (SBBs-dependent) and the backbone independent rotamer library allowed us to estimate the respective side chain conformational entropy. These results obtained with our backbone independent rotamer library are similar to those of other studies. Moreover, our results obtained with new rotamers libraries (SBBs-dependent) suggest a dependence between the side chain conformational entropy and the backbone conformation.
