12.  An Integrated Analysis System for Human Case‑Control Association studies with SNPs

Kyunghee Park[1], Kyusang Lee[2], Yeonsu Lee[3]

Keywords: SNP, statistical analysis, association study, automation, database, mass spectrometry

1     Introduction

Single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) are the most frequent variations in the human genome, and have attracted much interest in a wide range of biological and biomedical applications. Recent researches are focused on identifying disease susceptibility genes and finding associations between these markers and disease phenotypes using SNPs. For complex diseases, genome-wide scanning in association studies has been suggested, which is carried out by testing for differences in SNP allele frequencies between affected and unaffected individuals (case-control studies). Because complex diseases arise from multiple genes throughout the genome and contribution of each gene is small, many assays must be carried out to detect whole genotypes in a large sample size of individuals with tens of thousand SNPs. Due to the rapid advances of high-throughput, integrated technologies in genotyping of SNPs, bottlenecks have been shifted from the wet lab experiments to large data analyses and management generated by the experiments. Therefore it may be necessary to develop a robust, integrated and automated system for analyzing vast genotyping data to catch up with high-throughput genotyping system. We propose an integrated analysis system which is specialized for genome-wide scanning with large number of SNPs in case-control studies.

2     Materials and Methods

Experiments

We have conducted several case-control studies of complex diseases with Korean population. Our genotyping platform is MassARRAYTM (Sequenom, Inc.), which can detect mass differences of the products generated from primer extension by a matrix-assisted laser desorption/ionization time-of-flight (MALDI-TOF). It is semi-automated system and has its own ORACLE8i databases for data deposition and visualization software.

Programming language and platform

The analysis system was written in Perl and Oracle PL/SQL. All databases were built on ORACLE8i. It runs on WindowsNT server. 

3     Results and Future Works.

SNP analysis system: Analysis pipeline

We have been developing the prototype of the SNP analysis system based on the following pipeline (Fig. 1). The system is primarily focused on the integration of all information which is related to the samples, clinical information of each individual for different diseases, assays, sequences, SNP information, and experiment results. Furthermore we have developed sql scripts and SAS macro programs for further statistical analyses of case-control studies and quality control of genotyping results.

In the future, we are going to finish the integration of this system, and build a data warehouse. Also we will develop user interfaces, report generators, and visualization tools for mapping SNPs and analysis results, which are easy, simple and intuitive for the end users. In addition, an algorithm appropriate for genome-wide scanning approach in case-control studies will be developed to select minimal set of informative SNPs for the application to the molecular diagnosis of complex diseases.
