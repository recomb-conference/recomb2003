127. Automated Identification and Classification of alternative splice isoforms derived from EST data assembly

Gupta Shobhit[1],  Haas Stefan1,  Vingron Martin1

Keywords: alternative splicing, EST, splicenest, splice variants.

1    Introduction

Alternative splicing is currently envisaged to explain the vast disparity between the predicted human genome and the highly diverse proteome. One of the methodologies being adopted for the prediction of alternative splicing is with the use of Expressed Sequence Tag (EST) data. Assembly of the EST data into different clusters (genes) containing different contigs (alternative splice isoforms) and subsequent mapping of these contigs to the genome provides a good means of deciphering the gene structure. Such a mapping indicates probable exon-intron boundaries, thereby giving splicing related information (Genenest [1], Splicenest [2]). The current work associates a quality value to every exon-intron boundary and also to every exon predicted. With the use of this data we filter out alternative splice predictions based on reliable (high quality) exon-intron boundaries. Thus we remove false predictions due to incomplete splicing, contamination by genomic sequences or erroneous inclusion of ESTs from similar genes in other locations. Reducing the number of false positives while predicting alternative splicing will enable large-scale experimental verification of these predictions. The schema of our work can be split up into two main parts.

Ø       Automated Identification of Alternative Splice variants : Identification of Alternatively spliced variants is made by comparing the differences between various contigs of a cluster aligned to the same region of the genome. Quality values are then calculated for the predicited alternative splice variants based on a number of parameters like the EST coverage and the shared boundaries. Using such a quality value distribution, we can separately list reliable predictions of different types of alternative splicing like “skipped exon” events, “alternative donor-acceptor sites” events etc.

Ø        Classification of Splice variants : Correlating the annotation of ESTs with splice data may provide information regarding expression patterns of specific splice isoforms. We are focusing on the following aspects.

·          Tissue specific isoforms.

·          Stage specific isoforms.

·          Tumor specific isoforms.

Such an analysis of alternative splicing patterns and specificity will facilitate the detection of disease related splicing events, which could find wide applicability in pharmaceutical research.
