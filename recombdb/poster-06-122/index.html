122. Testing sets of genes defined by gene ontologies for differential expression in microarray experiments


Stefan Bentink1 and Rainer Spang



Keywords: gene ontology, microarray, class scoring, breast cancer

Introduction

Technologies like micro- or macroarrays allow insights into the regulation of several thousand genes at the same time. But the power of this new methods is also the source of problems concerning analysis and evaluation of the datasets obtained. On the one hand there is the difficulty in considering genes as significant in a multiple-testing background. On the other hand micorarray- experiments can produce confusing lists of hundreds of apparently deregulated genes.

In the present work a functional grid is laid on the expression-data. This functional classification is provided by the Gene Ontology (GO) database [#!go:2000!#]. Two different tests are proposed to find on the one hand an accumulation of induced genes in a GO-category (accumulation test) and on the other hand a contamination of the GO-category with a few genes that display moderately increased or decreased expression levels (contamination test).

Scoring of Gene Ontology categories

The first step to calculate GO-scores is a gene-wise two-sample t-test. The negative sum of log--values falling in a defined GO-category is taken as score. Apart from this method, which is well established in the literature [#!pavalidis:2002!#][#!zien:2000!#], a second score is introduced. It is based on the statistical test of Kolmogorov and Smirnov applied to the distribution of -values in a GO-category, and is similar to Tuckey's higher criticism score. The significance of scores is assessed by two permutation based tests. The null hypothesis of the first tests assumes that there are deregulated genes, which are however uniformly distributed over the Gene Ontology graph. The alternative is hence an accumulation of deregulated genes in a GO-category. The null hypothesis of the second test assumes that there are no deregulated genes in a GO-category at all. In this case we test for subtle changes of expression levels for the genes in a GO-category as a whole.

First results and experiences

To test the Gene Ontology approach, microarray-datasets from two different subtypes of human breast cancer are compared. The first dataset was obtained from Estrogen receptor positive samples, the second one came from Estrogen receptor negative tumor cells [#!west:2001!#]. The most important finding is, that our method provides insight, that can not be found by gene-wise screens, since the GO-scores are mainly driven by -values which do not indicate statistical significance in a gene-wise multiple testing context. This is proven by a set of genes found only in the functional context but not in a classical single gene approach. One interesting group is the GO-term mitosis. The differential expression of the genes in this group reflects the differences in the proliferative potential of the breast cancer types [#!gross:2002!#]. To enlarge the practical use, a graphical user-interface has been implemented which allows the molecular biologist for browsing and structuring results of microarray experiments.
