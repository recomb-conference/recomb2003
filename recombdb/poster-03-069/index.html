69. Annotating Significant Pairs of Transcription Factor Binding Sites in Regulatory DNA


Katja Rateitschak, Tobias Müller, Martin Vingron1 Katja.Rateitschak|Tobias.Mueller|Martin.Vingron@molgen.mpg.de



Keywords: recognition of genes and regulatory elements, transcription factor binding site, score matrix

Introduction

Vital processes in cells require a coordinated regulation of gene transcription. Gene transcription is controlled by protein complexes embedding transcription factors which bind to specific sequence patterns in regulatory DNA. These transcription factor binding sites (BS) occur in combinations of core sites and sites that are specific for groups of genes in consideration of order, distance and strand orientation. Therefore investigating the organization of BS in regulatory DNA is essential for understanding transcriptional regulation [1].

Binding sites are patterns of conserved nucleotides, and can be described by TRANSFAC matrices [2]. Searching BS with TRANSFAC matrices in regulatory DNA leads to many putative sites covering biological BS, but also many random hits. With the advent of many completely sequenced genomes, discriminating signal from noise becomes more and more important and difficult. The goal of the presented work is to improve the search power for BS by including additional information about typical BS pattern.

The new proposed score combines the ordinary profile score based on the TRANSFAC matrices together with a score based on pairs of BS. The latter score positively weights pairs of BS that tend to occur together in many regulatory DNA-sequences, in contrast to a random background model. The empirical BS pair frequencies result from our evaluation of a large dataset of orthologous genes. We describe our approach in Section 2. In Section 3 we apply our approach to muscle specific genes [3] and EPD-promoters [4].

Results and Outlook

We apply our method to two datasets. First we screen a set of muscle specific genes [3]. It consists of upstream sequences of 27 orthologous genes from man and mouse. We collect all positive scoring BS. Among the highest scoring pairs are the well known muscle specific pairs (SRF,SRF) and (MYOGENIN,MYOGENIN).

A second dataset is composed of non redundant EPD-promoters from man and mouse. Some identified high-scoring pairs occur exclusively in one EPD class: (SRF,SRF) regulates only contractile genes, and (ATF6,MAX/MYCMAX) regulates only genes relevant for amino acid metabolism. Other identified pairs, however, regulate genes of different EPD classes: (NRF,NRF) regulates protooncogenes, genes relevant for amino acid metabolism, and others.

In future work we will augment the scoring scheme to include information on the position, on strand orientation, and on the length of the intermediate sequence between binding sites. The challenges are the functional form of the score and the weighting of the individual pieces of information.
