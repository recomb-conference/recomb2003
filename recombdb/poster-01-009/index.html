9.  Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms: novel methods to predict their functional effects using Decision Trees and Support Vector Machines

Vidhya Gomathi Krishnan and David Robert Westhead[1]

Keywords: Single Nucleotide Polymorphism, Machine learning, prediction and mutations.

A common type of slight discrepancies that are prevalent in DNA sequences from the same species, involving deletions, insertions and substitutions are known as Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms (SNPs). Although SNPs can be classified as synonymous and non-synonymous based on whether they alter the encoded protein sequence or not, the non-synonymous type are the most important as they can alter the protein function. These SNPs play a key role in understanding variations in drug treatment responses to individuals at the molecular level and designing therapy. To understand the genetic and phenotypic variation, it is important to understand the effect of non-synonymous SNPs on protein structure and function.

Here, we present our work which is to focus on establishing a method to predict whether a given SNP affects the protein function or not, by Machine Learning (ML) tools. Various Machine Learning tools have advantages and disadvantages of their own. Therefore, in order to achieve a robust method a comparative study has been done here using Decision Trees (C4.5 [1]) and Support Vector Machines (SVM [2]). Prediction results from both the tools are presented here.

The methods are trained on extensive unbiased mutagenesis data from Lysozyme and Lac repressor proteins. Various cross-validation strategies on the data sets have been performed to check the prediction accuracy and compared with other available methods. The trained machine learning tools are then applied to make the predictions on the SNP data between the N2 strain and Hawaiian strain (CB4856 sequence) of the nematode worm Caenorhabditis elegans. Together with providing predictions for functional effects of C. elegans SNPs we provide a detailed comparative study of the utility and prediction accuracy of the two ML methods.
