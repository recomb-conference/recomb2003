150.  Trait: a knowledgebase to retrieve annotated and differentially expressed transcripts found in human skeletal muscle

Stefano Toppo[1], Nicola Cannata[2], Chiara Romualdi2, Paolo Laveder2, Gerolamo Lanfranchi2. Giorgio Valle2

Keywords: microarray, muscle, knowledgebase, transcripts and genes annotation

TRAIT [3] is a knowledgebase integrating information on human transcripts to related data from genome, proteins, ortholog genes, gene ontology, diseases and expression data based both on ESTs occurrence and microarray experiments.

It was initially built as a system to manage an EST-based gene discovery project on human skeletal muscle [2], that yielded to over 4,500 independent sequence clusters. Transcripts are anno­tated using automatic as well as manual procedures, aiming to link known transcripts to the main public domain resources and unknown transcripts to tables of predicted features.

In order to manage our data and to accomplish high quality annotation of the transcripts, the function of which is in many cases still unknown, we developed several bioinformatic tools performing many combined tasks. These tools make use of available programs such as BLAST as well as new algorithms developed in our bioinformatic laboratory such as AlphaSimp [1] that may be used to study conserved protein patterns.

In addition to TRAIT, a separate database has been specifically created to store the results obtained from filter hybridisation experiments (macroarrays) as well as microarrays. Currently, the expression patterns of ten different tissues are available, including the information about clones, spots and raw/normalised values.

These data are freely available at the following web addresses by means of a specifically designed retrieval system: http://muscle.cribi.unipd.it/features, http://muscle.cribi.unipd.it/expression.

All of the data are stored in a MySQL database and dedicated user friendly web interfaces have been developed to perform complex queries that allow the concurrent selection of different fields. Most fields are entered with the help of menus that only allow the definition of existing instances in the annotated database.

The main query form, dedicated to the retrieval of features, is divided into four main sections: transcripts and their ontology, genes, proteins and orthologs.

The free selection of the fields is automatically translated into SQL language, using OR operators to merge the searching criteria defined for each field. The resulting query is used to select suitable records from a TRAIT “view”, where all the original tables are merged into a single one, thus overcoming the difficulty of making “joins” using MySQL. Then the results are processed by a PHP script that sorts the hits on the basis of the number of satisfied criteria. For instance, if six criteria were defined in the query form and no entries satisfies them all, then instead of returning “no hits found” the PHP script will show all the entries satisfying at least five criteria, indicating which of the criteria were satisfied.

The latest version of TRAIT was produced to enhance the query capabilities and to allow an in-depth investigation of transcript features and gene expression levels. The user can perform different queries starting from the gene expression pattern. The results of over/under expressed genes may either be displayed or further investigated by using a dynamically generated query form. In this way the subset of over/under expressed genes may be further restricted to focus on particular annotated features.

For instance, it is possible to select the transcripts with a level of expression at least 10 times higher in skeletal muscle than in cardiac muscle; then it is possible to further restrict the query on the basis of the chromosomal location and on the presence of a particular domain on the translated protein, or on other features. Furthermore, TRAIT may also be accessed in the opposite way, starting from the query form of features and ontology of the transcripts. Also in this case the resulting ranked subsets may either be displayed or further restricted on the basis of their gene expression levels.
