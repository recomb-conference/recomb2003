46. Introducing Consistency-Based Hyperspace Alignments


Michael Sammeth, 1 Burkhard Morgenstern, 2 Jens Stoye


Keywords: multiple alignment, simultaneous alignment, consistency-based alignment, MSA, DCA, DIALIGN

Introduction

Synchronising all information of the input in a hyperspace lattice makes it possible to create multiple alignments finding the exact solution to the sum-of-pairs (SP) scoring function. Since this basic approach of simultaneous alignment is NP complete, it is not feasible for problems of realistic sizes. However, there exist some high quality heuristics for hyperspace alignments [1,2] that deliver solutions usually very close to optimality and thus still are treated as exact algorithms. But for SP-scoring strategy used, the algorithm fails to find good multiple alignments if global similarity is too weak [3,4].

To cope with more locally related sequence sets, several alternative approaches have been proposed [5,6,7]. These consistency-based methods assemle a global alignment from locally aligned segments and have shown promising results for problems where traditional strategies failed [4]. Furthermore, it has been shown that local fragments can be used to make the fast progressive alignments more robust [6]. But problems arise for this protocol, whenever local similarities are too low in a certain region of the input set. Herein, we will outline a novel approach to multiple sequence alignment that combines the strengths of segment-based and simultaneous multiple alignments.

Methods

The segment-to-segment comparison used in the protocol of DIALIGN [5] collects pairwise matching fragments for all input sequences. These local segments are weighted according to their matching grade and the qualities of other segments they are incompatible with. By considering alignments as consistent equivalence relations, the final alignment is created by defining the optimal path running from the source to the sink according to the highest scoring fragments assembled [8].

Afterwards, the consistent segments found are fed to the main alignment creation process in hyperspace. A stable re-implementation of the MSA software [1] was adapted to accept the pairwise fragments. Additionally to the Carillo-Lipman and the A*-like homing cost heuristics, conditions derived from selected pairwise fragments were applied to reduce the hyperspace. In order to align longer sequences, the efficient divide-and-conquer strategy for multiple sequence alignment as implemented in the program DCA [9] was adapted to accept the consistency conditions. The whole procedure is depicted in Fig. 1.

Speed-up and quality of the resulting consistency-based hyperspace alignment algorithm is benchmarked and compared to other alignment methods. The algorithm is also included in the new version of QAlign (see http://www.ridom.de/qalign), a graphical tool for constructing multiple alignments [10].
