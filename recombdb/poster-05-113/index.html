113. Two Maximum Step-Size Monte Carlo Simulation Combined with Multidimensional Histogram Techniques Applied to Engrailed Homeodomain and T4 Endonuclease V


J.-G. Siebers, 1 H. Yamaguchi, 2 A. Sarai 3



Keywords: generalized Monte Carlo, multidimensional histogram technique, direct readout, indirect readout, curvilinear free energy maps

Introduction

With this work we want to address two relatively separate problems: The first problem deals with a simple algorithm to generate entropic weight trajectories efficiently. First a configuration space volume is chosen which relates to a large maximum step size . A low negative energy threshold is used to bring the two macromolecular interfaces close to each other. Therefore, solvent effects are supposed to play a minor role at this stage and are taken implicitly into accout using a distant dependent dielectric. The generation of initial conditions takes about 50% of the simulational time and is therefore inefficient. To improve the efficiency about 99% of the production configurations are generated using a smaller step size . For all short trajectories we aimed at an overall acceptance ratio of about 50% giving a total acceptance ratio of about 75%. We applied the rigid rotor approximation and attached the coordinates to the DNA so that the inertia tensor is diagonal. Sampling was performed in cylindrical coordinates. All production data are binned into a coarse-grained 4-dimensional histogram, where three dimensions represent spatial coordinates [1, 2] while the fourth dimension represents energy space. This facilitates a posteriori weighting with the Boltzmann factor and separate integration over ranges of interest in each dimension. Integration over the radial and the weighted energy coordinates gives curvilinear free energy plots from the DNA perspective. Transformation of data to a spherical coordinate system attached to the protein and performing a corresponding integration gives curvilinear free energy plots from the protein perspective. The temperature is scaled with respect to curves measuring the heat capacity [3]. The second problem deals with the question of protein-DNA recognition mechanism. T4 Endonuclease V repairs thymine dimer (TD) damaged DNA and is supposed to recognize a kink introduced by TD [4]. Engrailed Homeodomain as a representative for homeodomains plays a fundamental role in diverse developmental processes and is highly sequence specific.

Software and files

From a structural point of view two DNA-protein compounds were used (PDB codes 2END and 3HDD). The program NUCGEN of the AMBER suite [5] was used to generate consensus B-DNA conformations of a given nucleic acid sequence. The programs LINK, EDIT and PARM of the AMBER suite were used to generate a topology and a separate force field file for the DNA-protein complex. The force field is based on PARM94 and uses a distant dependent dielectric to account for solvent effects. We have written an F77 program using parts of AMBER to calculate the interaction energies. The program is designed to generate short local trajectories using a simple hit-and-miss criterion for the energy [6]. The multiple trajectory file is analyzed and several files are generated in AVS/Express formats [7]. We have used the graphical programming interface of AVS/Express to generate scripts which display curvilinear free energy plots saved in the files described above.

Results and Discussion

The simulation results for Engrailed Homeodomain suggest a reaction pathway tracking the major groove of the DNA. There is a relatively sharp free energy transition between low and high energy regions suggesting a substructure of the protein fitting into the major groove. The protein model suggests a highly oriented protein allowing to define a rotational axis enabling hindered rotation about this axis. In contrast, the results for T4 Endonuclease V suggest no particular reaction pathway along an undamaged consensus B-DNA consisting of 36 base pairs. T4 Endonuclease V may recognize the specific conformation of DNA caused by the adduct, without tracking major groove of DNA. As for T4 Endonuclease V the approach offers the opportunity to verify the kink recognition assumption by computer experiments. The present method may be applied for protein-ligand and protein-protein docking analyses.
