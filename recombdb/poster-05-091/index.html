91. Analysis of the Structural Motifs at the Superfamily Level

S. Chakrabarti[1] and Dr. R. Sowdhamini1

Superfamily is a hierarchical classification, which contains proteins of different families and subfamilies, having similar kind of fold.  These proteins might have very low sequence identities but retain same fold through well-conserved secondary structural parts.  On the basis of criteria, like amino acid preference, solvent accessibility, secondary structure content, hydrogen bonding pattern, nonpolar and polar interaction, and residue packing, several conserved segmental parts of the proteins belonging to same superfamily have been identified.  These segments are termed as ‘structural motifs’.  A database of structural motifs for the superfamilies has been developed where the motifs are mapped on the alignments of superfamily proteins and interactive 3-dimensional views of the motifs in the superposed structure are displayed for better understanding and visualization. These motifs, along with their sequence and spatial orientation and preservation of various structural criteria, represent the conserved core structure of each superfamily.  One of the main purposes of this database is to provide important structural segments that can be projected as the minimum structural requirements for a new member to be considered part of a pre-existing superfamily.  This database of motifs can also be employed to design and rationalize protein engineering and folding experiments. The database is accessible from (http://www.ncbs.res.in/%7Efaculty/mini/SMoS/index.htm).
