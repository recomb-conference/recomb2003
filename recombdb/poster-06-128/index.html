128. Evaluation of gene expression patterns
based on EST clusters

Stefan A. Haas[1], Marc Bruning1, Martin Vingron1

Keywords: expressed sequence tags, EST, tissue-specificity, GeneNest, differential gene expression

1     Introduction

The large number of expressed sequence tags (ESTs) in public databases represents a major part of the entire set of human genes. Gene indices are clusters of ESTs related to the same gene that are used to reconstruct the sequence of transcripts by aligning ESTs. Additional information about the expression of those transcripts is covered in the sequence annotations. Most ESTs are annotated with information about the tissue, the tumor, or the developmental stage from which they are derived. However, the annotation of these features is often inconsistent, thus complicating the assignment of ESTs to specific tissues or stages. The high rate of misannotation further decreases the reliability of this data.

We performed a statistical analysis of the clustered ESTs in our GeneNest gene index database (http://genenest.molgen.mpg.de) in order to detect genes/transcripts that show a non-random expression pattern automatically. This analysis revealed large number of genes primarily expressed in specific tissues, developmental stages, and tumor tissue. In combination with expression data derived from microarrays this EST-based information will improve the detection of differentially expressed genes.
