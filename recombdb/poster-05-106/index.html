106.  New Database of Ligands and Protein Binding Sites from the PDB Integrated with CATH

Francisco Melo[1]

Keywords: ligands, protein binding sites, PDB, CATH

 

References

The Protein Data Bank (PDB) contains a large amount of proteins that have been solved with small ligands bound to them. This constitutes a rich source of information for the study of the specific requirements of protein sites to bind small molecules with a favorable free energy. The specific atomic composition and three-dimensional geometric restraints of protein binding sites for different ligands could be easily obtained from there. The development of accurate binding site descriptors in proteins constitutes a valuable tool to assist in the large-scale prediction and annotation of protein function in whole genomes.

In this work, an integrated database containing some processed and calculated protein/ligand information is described. The development, building and implementation of this new database is presented. The database contains all the three-dimensional coordinates of ligands bound to proteins available in the Protein Data Bank (PDB), along with the atomic coordinates of the protein sites in direct contact with those ligands. This information is fully integrated with the CATH database, allowing for the first time to analyze in a large scale the relationship between protein folds and ligand binding site requirements. It is expected that this database will constitute a useful tool for people working in the prediction of protein function from its structure. The database is accessible from the Internet through a web server located at: http://protein.bio.puc.cl
