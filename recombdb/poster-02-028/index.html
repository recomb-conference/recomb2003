28. Gabor Wavelets to Assess the Harmonic Content of DNA Sequences


Gunther J. L. Gerhardt, 1 Marcelo C. Pinto, 2 Tarso B. L. Kist



Keywords: Frequency analysis, wavelets, DNA

Introduction

There is a great amount of work assessing harmonic content of DNA in the form of Fourier Spectral Analysis of Correlation Functions (CF) calculated for DNA nucleotide sequences [1,2,3]. The Wavelet Transform (WT) has the advantage over Fourier Transform (FT) of giving information about the local (and not only global) frequency composition [4]. Along the next sections we will describe the CF and WT construction and apply them to a genomic sequence bin [Saccharomyces cerevisiae chromosome VII (GenBank NC001139)]. All data used here were taken from GenBank Database http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov.

Using this technique we can assess the frequency content at each segment position with the best possible resolution.
