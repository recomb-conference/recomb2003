  The Seventh Annual Conference on Research in Computational Molecular Biology (RECOMB 2003) will be organized by the Max Planck Institute for Molecular Genetics and the Berlin Center for Genome Based Bioinformatics in Berlin, Germany on April 10 -13, 2003. The conference will be held at the Maritim pro Arte Hotel, Berlin.Invited Speakers include Edward N. Trifonov, Christiane Nüsslein-Volhard, Árpád Furka, Andrew G. Clark, David Haussler, Arthur Lesk, Dieter Oesterheld, Terry Speed, and Kari Stefansson. About 35 other speakers will be chosen out of about 180 submitted abstracts. POSTERS reporting on original research (both theoretical and experimental) in all areas of computational molecular biology are sought. Typical but not exclusive topics of interest include:

  • Genomics 
  • Molecular sequence analysis 
  • Recognition of genes and regulatory elements 
  • Molecular evolution 
  • Protein structure 
  • Gene expression 
  • Gene networks 
  • Combinatorial libraries and drug design 
  • Computational proteomics 


ABSTRACT PREPARATION

Poster abstracts should be submitted electronically. The abstracts of accepted posters will be published in book form and will be available at the meeting. The abstracts, written in clear and reasonably grammatical English, must be submitted in exactly the same format as the example in the templates, and should fit on two pages, everything  included. Final posters may,  of course,  be in any format, as long as they fit onto a board of 1.32 m height and 0.90 m width (52.0 x 35.4 inches). 

Acceptance of an abstract is conditional on at least one of its authors preregistering for RECOMB 2003 electronically before February 3, 2003 (see http://www.recomb2003.de). 
 

Submission deadline for poster abstracts:  January 6, 2003
Notification of acceptance/rejection:  January 27,2003 

In the case of files we cannot open or which do not satisfy the format instructions, we will try to notify the authors within a few days of receipt to allow for resubmission. Authors who cannot comply with software or e-mail instructions should notify beziat@molgen.mpg.de well in advance of the deadline. 
 

ABSTRACT SUBMISSION

Submit a two-page poster abstract, preferably  the LaTeX source file (or Word file), AND either a postscript version or a .pdf version, or both, electronically to beziat@molgen.mpg.de.The files should be sent as attachments. The subject header of each message should identify the poster by the first author's surname and the type of file it contains. Authors submitting more than one abstract should disambiguate them using numerals, e.g. smith1.tex, smith1.ps, smith2.tex, smith2fig1.eps, smith2.pdf, smith2.ps, smith3.doc.

In order to present the posters in a clear manner, the abstracts should be classified in one of the following groups:
  • Genomics 
  • Molecular sequence analysis 
  • Recognition of genes and regulatory elements 
  • Molecular evolution 
  • Protein structure 
  • Gene expression 
  • Gene networks 
  • Combinatorial libraries and drug design 
  • Computational proteomics 
  • Others

Please, note the group your poster belongs to in the body of the mail.
